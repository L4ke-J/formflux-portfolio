Household Water Purifier

End-to-end product design from brief to manufacturing-ready prototype

Confidentiality notice: This project was delivered under a commercial agreement. Client identity, full product visuals, and proprietary specifications are not disclosed. The case study focuses on design process, methodology, and deliverable scope.

Overview

A complete product design engagement for a household water purification device — taking the project from an initial client brief through user research, concept development, industrial design, and engineering collaboration to a validated functional prototype ready for manufacturing handover.

The project was delivered within a defined commercial timeline and manufacturing budget, requiring tight coordination between design decisions and engineering constraints from the outset.

Design Process

Sole designer

The process followed a structured product development pipeline: brief clarification and competitive benchmarking, user research to validate assumptions, concept generation and selection, detailed industrial design, engineering collaboration, and prototype delivery.

Key design challenges included managing the internal volume constraints of the filtration system while maintaining a compact consumer-friendly form factor, specifying materials that met both food-safety standards and manufacturing cost targets, and designing for assembly efficiency to keep unit production costs within budget.

Engineering Collaboration

Close collaboration with the client's engineering team throughout the project ensured that design decisions were grounded in manufacturing reality. This included iterative reviews of internal component layouts, wall thicknesses, draft angles, snap-fit tolerances, and sealing requirements specific to water-contact products.

The engineering dialogue was particularly critical for the filter cartridge interface — a component where user serviceability, watertight sealing, and manufacturing repeatability all had to be resolved simultaneously.

Deliverables

The project concluded with a complete manufacturing-ready package:

  • Functional prototype validated against the product specification
  • Tooling-ready CAD files (SolidWorks) with full assembly documentation
  • CMF specification sheets — material grades, surface finishes, colour codes
  • Manufacturing guidance notes covering assembly sequence and QC checkpoints

Reflection

This project reinforced the value of early and continuous engineering integration in product design. The tightest design decisions — filter interface, internal packaging, material specification — were all moments where design intent and manufacturing constraint had to be resolved together, not sequentially.

Working as the sole designer on a commercially constrained timeline also sharpened the discipline of knowing when a design decision is "good enough" versus when it genuinely needs another iteration. Not every surface needs to be perfected; the ones that matter are those the user touches, sees, and trusts.
